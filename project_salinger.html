Life of J. D. Salinger

  • 1919, first of January: Born in Manhattan, New York
  • 1932: Was enrolled in a private scholl, McBurney School, where he wrote for the school newspaper and appeared in plays.
  • 1936: He graduated and started his freshman year at New York University, but dropped out and went to work for his father in Vienna
  • 1938: Left Austria just before it was anexed by Nazi Germany
  • 1939: Salinger attended the Columbia University School of General Studies, where he took a writing class taught by Whit Burnett, longtime editor of Story magazine, who plublished one of his stories and became his mentor
  • 1940: first stories were published in Story Magazine
  • 1941: He briefly worked on a Caribbean cruiser ship, serving as an activity director and possibly as a performer
  • 1942: started dating Oona O'Neil, who left him later to go out with Charles Chaplin
  • 1942: Hes was drafted into the army and saw combat and was present at Utah Beach on D-Day, in the Battle of the Bulge, and the Battle of HÃ¼rtgen Forest.
  • 1942: He met Ernest Hemingway. They became friends and started corresponding
  • 1946: Married with Sylvia Welter in Germany
  • 1948: The acclaimed story "A Perfect Day For Bananafish" appeared in The New Yorker
  • 1951: His most famous novel, "The Catcher in the Rye" is published and soon become a success
  • 1952: Became an adherent of Ramakrishna's Advaita Vedanta Hinduism, which advocated celibacy for those seeking enlightenment, and detachment from human responsibilities such as family
  • 1961: Published "Franny and Zooey"
  • 1963: Published "Raise High the Roof Beam, Carpenters"
  • 1965: He stopped publishing and would not do it again until his death
  • 1980: Gave his last interview
  • 2010: Died from natural causes in his home in New Hampshire
